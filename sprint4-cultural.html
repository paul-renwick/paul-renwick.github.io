Neuroplasticity & Growth Mindset

Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity is beneficial for those who believe that we are born with fixed abilities and/or intelligence. By understanding that the brain has plasticity to it, it allows us to understand that our capabilities and potentials are malleable. We are not born with a fixed ability, but rather, our abilities and intellectual skills are shaped through the pruning and reinforcing of connectivity amongst neurons. An understanding of Neuroplasticity can help us by overcoming any fixed biases regarding our capacity and instead feel that we are capable of achieving great things through practice and the strengthening/creation of neural pathways.

Studies into neuroplasticity have shown many ways in which we can benefit from this discovery, below I have listed some of the more interesting ways in which we can utilize this knowledge.

  • Mnemonic devices have been shown to enhance connectivity in the prefrontal parietal network and prevent some age-related memory loss
  • Learning a musical instrument increases connectivity between brain regions and helps form neural networks
  • Dancing reduces the risk of Alzheimer’s and increases neural connectivity

Growth mindset refers to the understanding that our abilities are not fixed and that we are able to develop our skillset and reach potentials through work and practice. This is important in helping us discard non-helpful beliefs such as talent trumping hard work.

Growth mindset is useful for my learning journey in that it gives me a tool to combat negative thoughts that revolve around others being better than me or not having the capabilities to learn to code. I don’t frequently have these thoughts but it is comforting to think that everyone started at the bottom and only got to being a proficient coder through practice and time.
